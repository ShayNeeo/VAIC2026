## 1. Purpose & Legal Basis

This policy establishes **zero-tolerance protection against retaliation** for employees who report violations, participate in investigations, or exercise Works Council rights. It implements the burden-of-proof shift mandated by the Whistleblower Protection Act (*Hinweisgeberschutzgesetz - HinSchG*) and Works Constitution Act (*Betriebsverfassungsgesetz - BetrVG*).

**Legal Basis:**
- *Hinweisgeberschutzgesetz (HinSchG)* § 13–15
- *Betriebsverfassungsgesetz (BetrVG)* § 15, § 78, § 103
- *Allgemeines Gleichbehandlungsgesetz (AGG)* § 16
- *EU Whistleblower Directive (EU) 2019/1937* Art. 19–21

---

## 2. Protected Activities

Protection applies to employees who:

| Activity | Legal Basis |
|----------|-------------|
| Report violations via internal/external whistleblower channel | HinSchG § 1(1), § 2 |
| Report violations to Works Council | BetrVG § 80(1) Nr. 2a |
| Participate in whistleblower investigation | HinSchG § 13(1) |
| Exercise Works Council mandate (candidate, member, deputy) | BetrVG § 15, § 103 |
| Participate in Works Council elections | BetrVG § 15 |
| File complaint under AGG (discrimination) | AGG § 16 |
| Refuse unlawful instructions | BetrVG § 78, BGB § 275 |
| Testify in labor court / conciliation proceedings | ArbGG, BetrVG § 76 |

## 3. Prohibited Retaliatory Actions

### 3.1 Explicit Prohibitions (HinSchG § 13, BetrVG § 103)

| Category | Examples | Legal Consequence |
|----------|----------|-------------------|
| **Termination** | Dismissal, non-renewal, constructive dismissal | Null & void (§ 103 BetrVG, § 13 HinSchG) |
| **Demotion/Transfer** | Lower grade, less favorable location, unfavorable shift | Reversible + damages |
| **Compensation** | Salary cut, bonus denial, benefit removal | Recoverable + damages |
| **Performance Management** | Unjustified negative review, PIP initiation | Inadmissible as evidence |
| **Career Development** | Training exclusion, project removal, promotion block | Discriminatory (§ 78 BetrVG) |
| **Work Conditions** | Hostile environment, isolation, increased surveillance | Injunctive relief + damages |
| **Reputation** | Negative references, blacklisting, public disparagement | Criminal (§ 15 HinSchG) |
| **Disciplinary** | Warning, suspension, formal reprimand | Void if retaliatory |

### 3.2 Subtle Retaliation (Also Prohibited)

- Exclusion from meetings/communications
- Unreasonable workload changes
- Denial of flexible work arrangements
- Micromanagement / excessive monitoring
- Social ostracism encouraged by management
- Delayed expense reimbursements
- Unfavorable schedule assignments

## 4. Burden of Proof Shift (HinSchG § 13(3) — Core Protection)

### 4.1 Statutory Presumption

**If an employee suffers adverse action within 1 year of protected activity:**

> The employer **must prove** the adverse action was **objectively justified** and **unrelated** to the protected activity.

### 4.2 Practical Application

| Scenario | Burden |
|----------|--------|
| Termination 3 months after whistleblower report | Employer proves: (a) legitimate reason existed before report, (b) same action would occur without report |
| Negative performance review after Works Council election | Employer proves: review criteria applied consistently, documented before protected activity |
| Transfer after AGG complaint | Employer proves: operational necessity, no alternative positions, transparent selection criteria |

### 4.3 Evidence Employer Must Produce

- Contemporaneous documentation of performance/conduct issues
- Decision-making trail showing legitimate business reason
- Consistency data (how similar cases treated)
- Timeline showing decision process predates protected activity

**Failure to produce → Retaliation presumed → Employee prevails.**

## 5. Reporting & Investigation of Retaliation

### 5.1 Reporting Channels

| Channel | Scope |
|---------|-------|
| Whistleblower portal (same as HinSchG) | All retaliation claims |
| Works Council direct | Council member retaliation, collective issues |
| AGG complaint body | Discrimination-based retaliation |
| Labor Court | Direct filing (expedited procedure) |

### 5.2 Investigation Standards (Same as SOP CORP-001)

- Independent investigator (no reporting line to accused)
- 30-day investigation timeline
- Burden of proof on employer
- Interim protective measures available (Labor Court § 62 ArbGG)

### 5.3 Interim Protection (Labor Court)

| Measure | When Available |
|---------|----------------|
| Suspension of termination | Pending main proceedings |
| Reinstatement (preliminary) | If termination likely retaliatory |
| Transfer freeze | Pending investigation |
| Negative action stay | If prima facie retaliation shown |

## 6. Remedies & Sanctions

### 6.1 Employee Remedies (HinSchG § 13–14, BetrVG § 103, AGG § 15)

| Remedy | Scope |
|--------|-------|
| **Reinstatement** | Full restoration to prior position |
| **Reversal of measure** | Transfer, demotion, compensation cut undone |
| **Material damages** | Lost wages, benefits, career opportunities |
| **Non-material damages** | Pain & suffering (Schmerzensgeld) — typically €5,000–50,000 |
| **Legal costs** | Full reimbursement (incl. attorney fees) |
| **Injunctive relief** | Court order stopping ongoing retaliation |

### 6.2 Employer Sanctions

| Violation | Sanction |
|-----------|----------|
| Retaliation against whistleblower | Fine up to €50,000 (HinSchG § 46) |
| Retaliation against Works Council member | Fine up to €25,000 (BetrVG § 119) |
| Obstruction of investigation | Fine up to €20,000 (HinSchG § 46) |
| Criminal retaliation (threats, coercion) | Prison up to 1 year (HinSchG § 15) |
| AGG violation | Damages + injunctive relief (AGG § 15, 21) |

### 6.3 Individual Manager Liability

- Personal liability for damages if personally directed retaliation
- Disciplinary action up to termination
- Professional license implications (where applicable)
- Criminal liability for coercion/threats (§ 240 StGB, HinSchG § 15)

## 7. Preventive Measures

### 7.1 Management Obligations

| Measure | Frequency | Owner |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| Anti-retaliation training | Annual + onboarding | HR / Compliance |
| Decision documentation protocol | Continuous | All managers |
| Retaliation risk assessment | Before any adverse action on protected person | HR / Legal |
| Works Council notification | Before action affecting council member | Management |
| Regular audit of adverse actions | Quarterly | Compliance / Works Council |

### 7.2 Decision Documentation Protocol

**Before any adverse employment action on protected person:**

1. **Document legitimate reason** with contemporaneous evidence
2. **Verify timeline** — reason must predate protected activity
3. **Consistency check** — compare to similar cases
4. **Legal review** — HR/Legal sign-off required
5. **Works Council informed** (if council member) — § 103 BetrVG
6. **Record retention** — minimum 3 years

### 7.3 Protected Person Registry

| Data | Purpose | Access |
|------|---------|--------|
| Whistleblower reporters (confidential) | Retaliation monitoring | Compliance only |
| Works Council members/deputies | Mandatory consultation | All management |
| AGG complainants | Retaliation monitoring | AGG body only |
| Investigation participants | Retaliation monitoring | Compliance only |

**Registry maintained in:** `.opencode-state/betriebsrat/design/protected-persons.json` (encrypted, access-controlled)
